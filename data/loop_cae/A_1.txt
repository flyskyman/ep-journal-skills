BACKGROUND: Catheter ablation of scar-dependent ventricular tachycardia (VT) is constrained by hemodynamic intolerance during mapping, long procedures, and high recurrence. Noninvasive prediction of reentrant circuits and optimal ablation targets before the procedure could overcome these limitations, but how faithfully circuits and ablation target sets predicted by MRI-based personalized heart digital twins correspond to invasively mapped clinical counterparts is unknown.

METHODS: In 18 patients with scar-dependent VT, a personalized computational heart digital twin was constructed from preprocedural contrast-enhanced cardiac MRI. VTs were induced in each model using rapid pacing protocols, and ablation targets were derived that would render all simulated VTs noninducible. Patients subsequently underwent invasive VT ablation guided by conventional criteria, including targeting of diastolic activity and optimal entrainment sites. Digital twin-predicted VT circuits, critical sites, and ablation target areas were compared head-to-head with the invasive clinical findings.

RESULTS: Invasive mapping induced 43 clinical VTs, and the digital twins simulated 92 VTs; diastolic activity was identified in 16 of 43 clinical VTs (37.2%). For detection of critical VT sites, the digital twin achieved a sensitivity of 81.3% and specificity of 83.8%, with a high negative predictive value (98.8%) but low positive predictive value (21.7%). Spatial agreement between clinical critical sites and digital twin primary predicted sites at the AHA-segment level was moderate (kappa=0.46+/-0.32; P<=0.001). Where ablation was attempted at a digital twin-predicted site, VT was terminated in 4 of 5 cases (80%), and 426 of 709 lesions (60.1%) were delivered within 5 mm of a predicted target. Using conventional mapping criteria, 54.0%+/-28.9% of the digital twin-predicted target area was ablated per patient.

CONCLUSIONS: MRI-based personalized heart digital twins reproduce many features of clinically observed VT circuits and ablation targets, with high sensitivity and negative predictive value but limited positive predictive value and only moderate spatial agreement, reflecting current constraints in spatial resolution. These findings demonstrate the potential of digital twin technology to noninvasively guide catheter ablation for scar-dependent VT and warrant prospective evaluation of digital twin-directed targeting.
