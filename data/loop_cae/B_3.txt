Background: miR1 (microRNA-1), encoded by the miR1-1 and miR1-2 genes, is the most abundant cardiac microRNA and is essential for cardiac development and physiology. Hearts from patients with atrial fibrillation or acute myocardial infarction show a greater than 75% reduction in miR1 expression. Whether miR1 deficiency is a primary etiological driver of cardiac remodeling, rather than a downstream consequence, remains unknown.

Methods: We crossbred miR1-1 and miR1-2 knockout lines to generate mice (genotypes miR1-1+/-:miR1-2-/- or miR1-1-/-:miR1-2+/-) in which total cardiac miR1 was reduced to approximately 25% of wild-type levels (75% knockdown, 75%KD), modeling the deficiency observed in diseased human hearts. Wild-type littermates served as comparators. Cardiomyocytes and whole hearts were characterized by echocardiography, electrocardiography, patch clamping, optical mapping, RNA sequencing, and proteomics. To test reversibility, 75%KD mice received bi-weekly intravenous tail-vein injections of miR1 mimics over 6 weeks.

Results: Adult 75%KD hearts developed overt cardiac dysfunction, with decreased ejection fraction and fractional shortening. Electrocardiography revealed prolonged QRS and QT intervals and high susceptibility to arrhythmias. 75%KD cardiomyocytes exhibited prolonged action potentials with impaired repolarization and impaired excitation-contraction coupling, accompanied by reduced Na+ current, reduced transient outward K+ current, and elevated L-type Ca2+ current relative to wild-type. Transcriptomic and proteomic profiling showed negative regulation of cardiac muscle contraction and ion channel activity together with positive enrichment of smooth muscle contraction genes. miR1 deficiency dysregulated a broad gene network, with direct miR1 targets propagating widespread indirect changes. Bi-weekly miR1 mimic delivery for 6 weeks significantly improved ejection fraction and fractional shortening in 75%KD hearts; however, the hearts remained susceptible to arrhythmias.

Conclusions: miR1 deficiency acts as a primary etiological factor that induces cardiac remodeling by disrupting cardiac regulatory homeostasis. The incomplete rescue, with persistent arrhythmic vulnerability despite restored contractile function, indicates that achieving stable and appropriately titrated microRNA levels is critical and underscores a narrow therapeutic window for miRNA-based therapy in cardiovascular disease.
