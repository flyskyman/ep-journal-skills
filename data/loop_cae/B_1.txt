BACKGROUND: Catheter ablation of scar-dependent ventricular tachycardia (VT) is limited by hemodynamic instability, prolonged procedures, and high recurrence, in part because reentrant circuits and optimal ablation targets cannot be reliably identified noninvasively before the procedure. MRI-based personalized heart digital twins may predict VT circuits and ablation sites, but their correspondence with invasively mapped clinical counterparts is unknown. We sought to compare digital twin-predicted VT circuits and ablation targets head-to-head with invasive findings.

METHODS: In 18 patients with scar-dependent VT, a personalized digital twin was constructed from preprocedural contrast-enhanced cardiac MRI. VT was simulated using rapid pacing protocols, and a set of ablation targets predicted to terminate all simulated VTs was derived for each model. Patients subsequently underwent invasive VT ablation, including targeting of diastolic activity and optimal entrainment sites. Digital twin-predicted VT circuits, critical sites, and ablation target areas were compared with the invasive clinical mapping and ablation results.

RESULTS: A total of 43 clinical VTs were induced, and 92 VTs were simulated in the digital twins. Diastolic activity was present in 16 of 43 clinical VTs (37.2%). For detection of critical VT sites, the digital twin had a sensitivity of 81.3%, specificity of 83.8%, positive predictive value of 21.7%, and negative predictive value of 98.8%. Agreement at the AHA-segment level between clinical VT critical sites and digital twin primary predicted sites was moderate (kappa=0.46+/-0.32; P<=0.001). VT was terminated by ablation at a digital twin-predicted site in 4 of 5 (80%) cases in which this was attempted. Overall, 426 of 709 lesions (60.1%) were delivered within 5 mm of a predicted target site, and 54.0%+/-28.9% of the digital twin-predicted area was ablated per patient using conventional mapping criteria.

CONCLUSIONS: MRI-based personalized heart digital twins reproduce many features of clinically observed VT circuits and ablation targets, with high sensitivity and negative predictive value but low positive predictive value and only moderate spatial agreement, reflecting current limitations in spatial resolution. These findings demonstrate the potential of digital twin technology to noninvasively guide catheter ablation for scar-dependent VT.
