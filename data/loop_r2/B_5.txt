Background: Thermal energy sources (radiofrequency [RF] and cryoablation) are the established modalities for pulmonary vein isolation (PVI) in paroxysmal atrial fibrillation (AF) and are regarded as effective and safe. However, long-term freedom from arrhythmia remains limited, and complications can arise from thermal spread into adjacent non-target structures such as the oesophagus and phrenic nerve. Pulsed field ablation (PFA) is a non-thermal, tissue-selective modality that may offer improved safety and efficacy, but head-to-head randomized evidence against RF ablation in drug-resistant paroxysmal AF is lacking.

Objective: To describe the design and rationale of the BEAT PAROX-AF trial, which tests the hypothesis that PFA is superior to RF ablation in both efficacy and safety for the treatment of drug-resistant paroxysmal AF.

Methods: BEAT PAROX-AF is a European multicentre, open-label, superiority randomized controlled trial conducted at 9 high-volume clinical centres across 5 countries. A total of 292 patients with drug-refractory paroxysmal AF were enrolled and randomized 1:1 to two parallel arms: PFA using the FARAPULSE Endocardial Ablation System (Boston Scientific) versus RF ablation performed according to the CLOSE protocol with a contact-force-sensing catheter (SmartTouch, CARTO mapping system, Biosense Webster). The primary endpoint is recurrence of atrial arrhythmia at 1 year. The major secondary safety endpoint is the occurrence of acute (<7 days) procedure-related serious adverse events, pulmonary vein stenosis, or atrio-oesophageal fistula up to 12 months. Five pre-specified sub-studies will evaluate (1) oesophageal safety, (2) silent cerebral lesions, (3) effects on the cardiac autonomic nervous system, (4) durability of PVI assessed during redo/repeat ablation procedures, and (5) atrial and ventricular function.

Results: The trial commenced on 27 December 2021, and recruitment concluded on 17 January 2024. Outcome results are expected in mid-2025; no efficacy or safety results are reported in this protocol paper.

Conclusion: BEAT PAROX-AF is designed to provide the first head-to-head randomized comparison of PFA and RF ablation in drug-resistant paroxysmal AF. Its findings, together with five dedicated mechanistic and safety sub-studies, are intended to clarify whether PFA is the superior ablation strategy and to inform the optimal treatment approach for this population.
