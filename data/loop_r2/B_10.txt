Background: Left atrial appendage closure (LAAC) is an established option for stroke prevention in patients with non-valvular atrial fibrillation. Anticoagulation-related spinal epidural hematoma (SEH) and device-related thrombosis (DRT) are rare but serious post-LAAC complications, and management guidance is limited—particularly when both occur sequentially in a single patient, pulling antithrombotic therapy in opposite directions.

Case: An 86-year-old woman with persistent non-valvular atrial fibrillation, chronic heart failure, and multiple high-risk features presented with recurrent chest tightness for six months. She underwent successful LAAC and was started on edoxaban 30 mg once daily. Six weeks after the procedure she developed an anticoagulation-related SEH; anticoagulation was discontinued immediately and conservative management was pursued, with subsequent resolution of the hematoma and improvement in muscle strength. She was transitioned to antiplatelet therapy with clopidogrel 75 mg once daily. At the 6-month follow-up, transthoracic echocardiography and CT angiography identified a large left atrial DRT. Anticoagulation was reinitiated using low-molecular-weight heparin bridging to warfarin, with the INR maintained at a target of 2.0–2.5. Follow-up imaging at 8 months demonstrated significant resolution of the left atrial thrombus, and no further major adverse events—no ischemic stroke and no bleeding—occurred over the remaining follow-up.

Conclusion: This case illustrates the opposing therapeutic demands of bleeding and thrombotic complications after LAAC in a high-risk patient. Antithrombotic strategy should be individualized and dynamically adjusted, and meticulous perioperative procedural quality control together with structured follow-up within key time windows is essential to minimize complications and optimize net clinical benefit.
