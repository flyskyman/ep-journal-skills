BACKGROUND: Left atrial appendage closure (LAAC) is generally contraindicated in the presence of LAA thrombus, and the limited prior experience has relied almost exclusively on transesophageal echocardiography (TEE) for guidance. Although intracardiac echocardiography (ICE) is increasingly used for LAAC, its feasibility in patients with LAA thrombus has not been established.

OBJECTIVE: To assess the feasibility and short-term safety of ICE-guided LAAC with the LAmbre device in patients with LAA thrombus.

METHODS: In this single-arm observational feasibility study, consecutive patients with persistent LAA thrombus despite anticoagulation, or with contraindications to anticoagulation, underwent a modified ICE-guided LAAC procedure between June 2021 and April 2023. A cerebral protection device was used during implantation when feasible. Periprocedural events and follow-up clinical and imaging outcomes were recorded.

RESULTS: Twelve patients were enrolled (mean age 65 +/- 7 years; 92% male); 10 had persistent LAA thrombus despite anticoagulation and 2 had contraindications to anticoagulation. Thrombus was located at the apex in 6 patients, the body in 3, and the ostium in 3. The LAmbre device was successfully implanted under ICE guidance in all 12 patients (100%). A cerebral protection device was used in 11 patients, and no thrombotic material was retrieved in any of them. No severe periprocedural complications occurred. All patients completed TEE follow-up, which showed no device-related thrombus and no peridevice leak >3 mm. Over a median follow-up of 147 days (IQR 80-306), there were no strokes or transient ischemic attacks, no systemic embolism, and no major bleeding events.

CONCLUSIONS: In this initial experience, ICE-guided LAAC with the LAmbre device was feasible and was not associated with severe complications or thromboembolic events in patients with LAA thrombus when performed by experienced operators. These preliminary findings warrant confirmation in larger studies.
