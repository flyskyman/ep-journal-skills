Background
Vascular access-site complications are the leading cause of delayed discharge after pulmonary vein isolation (PVI), and the time required for femoral venous hemostasis remains a key barrier to same-day discharge at a time when healthcare facility overcrowding is an increasing concern. We evaluated whether a suture-mediated vascular closure device could enable a safe ambulatory PVI workflow.

Methods
PRO-PVI was a prospective single-arm observational study enrolling 50 patients scheduled for PVI. Femoral venous access was closed with the Perclose Proglide suture-mediated closure device within a same-day-discharge workflow. A 1:1 propensity-matched control cohort treated with the usual workflow served as the comparator for discharge timing. Endpoints comprised feasibility (proportion discharged on the day of the procedure), efficacy (acute closure rate, time to hemostasis, time to ambulation, and time to discharge), and safety (vascular complications at 30 days), together with patient satisfaction and a direct- and indirect-cost analysis.

Results
Same-day discharge was achieved in 96% of patients, and device deployment was successful in 100%. Immediate hemostasis (<1 min) was obtained in 30 of 50 patients (62.5%). Mean time to discharge was 5:48 +/- 1:03 h with the closure device versus 10:16 +/- 1:21 h in the matched cohort (P < 0.0001). No major vascular complication occurred through 30 days. Patient satisfaction with the postoperative recovery period was high, and the cost analysis demonstrated a neutral impact relative to the standard of care.

Conclusions
Suture-mediated femoral venous closure after PVI enables safe same-day discharge within approximately 6 h in 96% of patients, halving postoperative recovery and discharge time with no major vascular complications, improved patient satisfaction, and a cost-neutral impact. This ambulatory approach could help reduce healthcare facility overcrowding.
