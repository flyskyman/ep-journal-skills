BACKGROUND: The HeartMate 3 (HM3) fully magnetically levitated left ventricular assist device (LVAD) has markedly reduced thromboembolic events compared with the HeartMate II, yet non-surgical bleeding remains a significant source of morbidity. The standard post-implant antithrombotic regimen combines warfarin with aspirin (ASA), but whether ASA adds benefit over warfarin alone is unestablished. We compared the impact of warfarin alone versus warfarin plus ASA on bleeding outcomes in HM3 recipients.

METHODS: We performed a systematic review and meta-analysis searching MEDLINE, Embase, and the Cochrane Library for randomized and observational studies comparing warfarin alone with warfarin plus ASA in HM3 LVAD patients. Binary endpoints were pooled as risk ratios (RR) with 95% confidence intervals (CI) using a random-effects model. The primary endpoint was non-surgical bleeding; secondary endpoints were gastrointestinal (GI) bleeding and all-cause mortality.

RESULTS: Five studies (1 randomized controlled trial and 4 observational; 869 patients, 76.2% male) were included, of whom 424 (48.8%) received warfarin alone. Compared with the combined regimen, warfarin alone was associated with a significantly lower risk of non-surgical bleeding (RR 0.30, 95% CI 0.09-0.95; p=0.04) and GI bleeding (RR 0.26, 95% CI 0.12-0.58; p<0.001). All-cause mortality did not differ between groups (RR 1.02, 95% CI 0.45-2.32; p=0.963).

CONCLUSION: In HM3 LVAD patients, warfarin alone was associated with reduced non-surgical and GI bleeding compared with combined warfarin plus ASA, without an increase in mortality. These findings support omitting aspirin as a strategy to lower bleeding risk and warrant confirmation in dedicated randomized trials.
