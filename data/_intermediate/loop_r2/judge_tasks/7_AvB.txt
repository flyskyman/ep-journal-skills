BACKGROUND: Patients undergoing orthotopic transcatheter tricuspid valve replacement (TTVR) frequently have a cardiac implantable electronic device (CIED) lead crossing the tricuspid valve. The clinical, procedural, and lead-related outcomes of TTVR performed in the presence of such a jailed transvalvular lead have not been characterized.

OBJECTIVES: This study sought to evaluate the safety and effectiveness of orthotopic TTVR in patients with a transvalvular CIED lead and to assess subsequent lead function.

METHODS: We analyzed all consecutive patients undergoing orthotopic TTVR enrolled in TRIPLACE ([registration]), a multicenter, multinational prospective observational registry. Patients were stratified by the presence of a CIED lead traversing the tricuspid valve. Lead failure was defined as structural or electrical malfunction requiring insertion of a new lead or CIED. Changes in lead function were assessed in the subset of patients with recorded pacemaker lead parameter data.

RESULTS: Among 395 patients, 104 (26.3%) had a transvalvular CIED lead and 291 (73.7%) did not. Procedural success, symptomatic improvement, and 30-day mortality were comparable between groups. Mild-or-less residual tricuspid regurgitation was less frequent in the CIED-lead group (82.6% vs 91.4%; P<0.041), and moderate-or-greater paravalvular leak was more frequent (17.1% vs 7.1%; P<0.017). Over a median follow-up of 183 days, lead failure occurred in 5.8% of patients, with only modest changes in pacing thresholds. There was no significant increase in adverse events or mortality at 30 days.

CONCLUSIONS: Orthotopic TTVR can be performed safely and effectively in patients with a transvalvular CIED lead, with a low rate of lead failure. However, jailed leads were associated with more significant paravalvular leak and residual tricuspid regurgitation. Close CIED follow-up and consideration of alternative pacing strategies are warranted, particularly in pacing-dependent patients.
