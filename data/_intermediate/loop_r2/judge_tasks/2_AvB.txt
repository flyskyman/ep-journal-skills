=== Abstract X ===
BACKGROUND: Distinguishing nearfield from farfield electrograms is essential for accurate mapping and ablation of arrhythmias and for confirming pulmonary vein isolation (PVI). However, this assessment remains subjective, relying on the operator's perception of electrogram "sharpness," and a quantitative, objective metric is lacking.

OBJECTIVE: To test whether electrogram peak frequency (PF) separates nearfield from farfield signals and can confirm PVI intraprocedurally.

METHODS: In this prospective mapping study, left atrial and pulmonary vein (PV) PF maps were generated before and after PVI in 30 patients with atrial fibrillation (AF) undergoing the procedure. Patients were divided into three sequential cohorts of 10. Cohort 1 (first 10 paroxysmal AF patients) was used to derive a PF cutoff differentiating farfield from nearfield electrograms; cohort 2 (10 paroxysmal AF) and cohort 3 (10 persistent AF) were used for validation. Diagnostic accuracy of the derived cutoff was assessed against the established nearfield/farfield classification.

RESULTS: PF was consistently lower in farfield than in nearfield electrograms. A PF cutoff of 300 Hz best differentiated the two signal types. Using this cutoff, sensitivity for identifying nearfield signals was 93.2% (95% CI 81.3-98.6) in cohort 1, 90.0% (95% CI 76.3-97.2) in cohort 2, and 98.6% (95% CI 90.1-99.7) in cohort 3, with a specificity of 100.0% (95% CI 98.2-100.0). Across all patients, the area under the ROC curve was 0.99 (95% CI 0.97-1.00).

CONCLUSION: Electrogram peak frequency reliably distinguishes nearfield from farfield electrograms and improves recognition of PVI. PF-guided mapping and ablation strategies warrant further study as a means to improve ablation outcomes.
