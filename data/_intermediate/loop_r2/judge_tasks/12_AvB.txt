BACKGROUND: The HeartMate 3 (HM3) fully magnetically levitated left ventricular assist device (LVAD) has markedly reduced thromboembolic events compared with the HeartMate II, yet non-surgical bleeding remains a significant source of morbidity. The standard post-implant antithrombotic regimen combines warfarin with aspirin (ASA), but whether ASA adds benefit over warfarin alone is unestablished. We compared the impact of warfarin alone versus warfarin plus ASA on bleeding outcomes in HM3 recipients.

METHODS: We performed a systematic review and meta-analysis searching MEDLINE, Embase, and the Cochrane Library for randomized and observational studies comparing warfarin alone with warfarin plus ASA in HM3 LVAD patients. Binary endpoints were pooled as risk ratios (RR) with 95% confidence intervals (CI) using a random-effects model. The primary endpoint was non-surgical bleeding; secondary endpoints were gastrointestinal (GI) bleeding and all-cause mortality.

RESULTS: Five studies (1 randomized controlled trial and 4 observational; 869 patients, 76.2% male) were included, of whom 424 (48.8%) received warfarin alone. Compared with the combined regimen, warfarin alone was associated with a significantly lower risk of non-surgical bleeding (RR 0.30, 95% CI 0.09-0.95; p=0.04) and GI bleeding (RR 0.26, 95% CI 0.12-0.58; p<0.001). All-cause mortality did not differ between groups (RR 1.02, 95% CI 0.45-2.32; p=0.963).

CONCLUSION: In HM3 LVAD patients, warfarin alone was associated with reduced non-surgical and GI bleeding compared with combined warfarin plus ASA, without an increase in mortality. These findings support omitting aspirin as a strategy to lower bleeding risk and warrant confirmation in dedicated randomized trials.

Background: The HeartMate 3 (HM3) is a fully magnetically levitated left ventricular assist device (LVAD) that markedly reduces thromboembolic events compared with the HeartMate II; however, bleeding complications remain a major source of morbidity. The conventional antithrombotic regimen after HM3 implantation combines warfarin with aspirin (ASA), yet the optimal strategy is not established and the incremental value of ASA is uncertain. We performed a systematic review and meta-analysis to assess the impact of warfarin alone versus combined warfarin plus ASA on bleeding in HM3 LVAD recipients.

Methods: MEDLINE, Embase, and the Cochrane Library were searched for randomized controlled trials and observational studies comparing warfarin monotherapy with warfarin plus ASA in patients supported by the HM3 LVAD. Binary outcomes were pooled as risk ratios (RR) with 95% confidence intervals (CI) using a random-effects model. The primary endpoint was non-surgical bleeding; secondary endpoints were gastrointestinal (GI) bleeding and all-cause mortality.

Results: Five studies (1 randomized controlled trial and 4 observational studies) comprising 869 patients were included. Of these, 424 (48.8%) received warfarin alone and 662 (76.2%) were male. Compared with the combined warfarin plus ASA regimen, warfarin alone was associated with a significant reduction in non-surgical bleeding (RR 0.30, 95% CI 0.09-0.95; p=0.04) and in GI bleeding (RR 0.26, 95% CI 0.12-0.58; p<0.001). All-cause mortality did not differ between the two strategies (RR 1.02, 95% CI 0.45-2.32; p=0.963).

Conclusions: In patients supported by the HeartMate 3 LVAD, warfarin alone was associated with a lower risk of non-surgical and gastrointestinal bleeding than combined warfarin plus aspirin therapy, with no difference in all-cause mortality. These findings support reconsideration of routine aspirin use in HM3 recipients and warrant confirmation in adequately powered randomized trials.
