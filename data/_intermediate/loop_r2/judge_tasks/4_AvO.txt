=== Abstract X ===
AIMS
Vascular access-site complications are the leading cause of delayed discharge after pulmonary vein isolation (PVI), and prolonged femoral venous hemostasis time is a principal barrier to same-day discharge at a time when healthcare facility overcrowding is an increasing concern. However, a femoral venous closure strategy that reliably enables an ambulatory PVI workflow has not been established. We aimed to evaluate the feasibility, efficacy, and safety of suture-mediated femoral venous closure to support same-day discharge after PVI.

METHODS AND RESULTS
In this prospective single-arm observational study (PRO-PVI), 50 patients scheduled for PVI underwent femoral venous access-site closure with the Perclose ProGlide suture-mediated closure device within an ambulatory same-day-discharge workflow. A 1:1 propensity-matched cohort managed with the usual workflow served as a control for time-to-discharge comparison. Endpoints were feasibility (proportion discharged on the day of the procedure), efficacy (acute closure rate, time to hemostasis, time to ambulation, time to discharge), and safety (vascular complications at 30 days), together with patient satisfaction and direct plus indirect cost. Same-day discharge was achieved in 96% of patients, and device deployment was successful in 100%. Immediate hemostasis (<1 min) was obtained in 30/50 patients (62.5%). Mean time to discharge was markedly shorter than in the matched control cohort (5:48 +/- 1:03 h vs 10:16 +/- 1:21 h, P < 0.0001), approximately halving post-procedural recovery time. No major vascular complication occurred through 30 days. Patient satisfaction with the post-operative course was high, and cost analysis showed a neutral impact relative to the standard of care.

CONCLUSION
Suture-mediated femoral venous closure after PVI enabled safe same-day discharge within approximately 6 h in 96% of patients, halving post-procedural recovery and discharge time without major vascular complications, while improving patient satisfaction at cost-neutral impact. These findings, from a single-arm study with a propensity-matched comparator, support suture-mediated venous closure as a strategy for ambulatory PVI that may help reduce healthcare facility overcrowding and warrant confirmation in randomized trials.

=== Abstract Y ===
AIMS: The leading reason for delayed discharge after pulmonary vein isolation (PVI) is vascular complications. This study aimed to evaluate feasibility, safety, and efficacy of the Perclose Proglide™ suture-mediated vascular closure in ambulatory PVI, report complications, patient satisfaction, and cost of this approach.
METHODS AND RESULTS: Patients scheduled for PVI were enrolled prospectively in an observational design. Feasibility was assessed as % discharged the day of procedure. Efficacy was analysed as acute access site closure rate, time to reach haemostasis, time to ambulate, and time to discharge. Safety analysis consisted of vascular complications at 30 days. Cost analysis was reported using direct and indirect cost analysis. A 1:1 propensity matched control cohort was used for comparing time to discharge to usual workflow. Of 50 enrolled patients, 96% were discharged on the same day. 100% of devices were successfully deployed. Immediate (<1 min) haemostasis was reached in 30 patients (62.5%). Mean time to discharge was 5:48 ± 1:03 h (vs. 10:16 ± 1:21 h in the matched cohort, P < 0.0001). Patients reported high level of satisfaction with the post-operative time. No major vascular complication occurred. Cost analysis showed a neutral impact compared to the standard of care.
CONCLUSION: The use of the closure device for femoral venous access after PVI led to safe discharge of patients within 6 h from the intervention in 96% of the population. This approach could minimize the overcrowding of healthcare facilities. The gain in post-operative recovery time improved patients' satisfaction and balanced the economic cost of the device.
