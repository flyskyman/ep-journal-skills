BACKGROUND: Leadless pacemakers (LPMs) avoid transvenous leads and subcutaneous pockets, lowering lead- and pocket-related complications. Whether neighborhood income influences in-hospital outcomes after LPM implantation remains poorly characterized.

METHODS: We performed a retrospective analysis of the National Inpatient Sample (2018-2022), identifying adult LPM implantations and applying discharge weights to estimate national totals. Admissions were stratified into quartiles of ZIP-code-based median household income (Q1, lowest, to Q4, highest). Multivariable logistic regression, adjusted for demographics, comorbidities, payer, and hospital region, generated adjusted odds ratios (aORs) for in-hospital mortality, pericardial complications, bleeding, transfusion, venous thromboembolism (VTE), renal complications, and device removal/revision.

RESULTS: Among 41,985 weighted admissions (8397 unweighted), Q1 patients were younger (median 77.0 vs 81.0 years; p<0.001) with more diabetes (44.9% vs 33.8%) and end-stage renal disease (43.4% vs 36.6%). Crude mortality was highest in Q1 (4.7%), but after adjustment Q2 had higher odds of in-hospital death versus Q1 (aOR 1.517; 95% CI 1.115-2.064; p=0.008). Pericardial complications rose with income (Q1 3.2% vs Q4 4.1%); Q4 had higher adjusted odds versus Q1 (aOR 1.753; 95% CI 1.244-2.469; p=0.001). Q4 had lower adjusted odds of bleeding (aOR 0.788; 95% CI 0.633-0.982; p=0.034) and transfusion (aOR 0.645; 95% CI 0.494-0.842; p=0.001). VTE was more likely in Q2 and Q4 versus Q1. Length of stay was longer in Q1 (6.0 vs 5.0 days; p<0.001).

CONCLUSION: Neighborhood income shows endpoint-specific, rather than uniform, associations with in-hospital LPM outcomes. These patterns may inform peri-procedural counseling and institutional review.

OBJECTIVE: To evaluate the association between ZIP-code-based median household income and in-hospital outcomes after LPM implantation in the United States.

Q4 had lower adjusted odds of bleeding (aOR 0.788; 95% CI, 0.633 to 0.982; p = 0.034) and transfusion (aOR 0.645; 95% CI, 0.494 to 0.842; p = 0.001).
