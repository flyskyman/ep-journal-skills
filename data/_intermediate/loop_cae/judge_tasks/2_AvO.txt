BACKGROUND: Fabry disease (FD) is an X-linked lysosomal storage disorder caused by α-Gal A (α-galactosidase A) deficiency, resulting in multiorgan accumulation of sphingolipid, namely globotriaosylceramide. This triggers ventricular myocardial hypertrophy, fibrosis, and inflammation, driving arrhythmia and sudden death. Atrial fibrillation is common, yet the cellular mechanisms accounting for this are unknown.
METHODS: To address this, we conducted ECG analysis from a large cohort of 115 adults with FD at varying cardiomyopathy stages. ECG P-wave characteristics were compared with non-FD controls. Cellular contractile and electrophysiological function were examined in a novel atrial cellular FD model developed and imputed into in silico atrial models to provide insight into mechanisms of arrhythmia. Induced pluripotent stem cells were genome-edited using Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats-Cas9 to introduce the GLA p.N215S variant and differentiated into induced pluripotent stem cell-derived atrial cardiomyocytes (iPSC-CMs). Contraction, calcium handling, and electrophysiology experiments were conducted. Bi-atrial in silico models were developed with cellular changes as in GLA p.N215S iPSC-CMs.
RESULTS: ECG analysis demonstrated P-wave duration and PQ interval shortening in FD adults before the onset of cardiomyopathy. Patients with FD exhibited a higher incidence of premature atrial contractions and increased risk of atrial fibrillation compared with healthy controls. GLA p.N215S iPSC-CMs were deficient in α-Gal A and exhibited globotriaosylceramide accumulation. Atrial GLA p.N215S iPSC-CMs demonstrated a more positive diastolic membrane potential, faster action potential upstroke velocity, greater incidence of delayed afterdepolarizations, greater contraction force, and alterations in calcium handling compared with wild-type iPSC-CMs. Simulations with these changes in the in silico models resulted in similar P-wave morphology changes to those seen in early FD cardiomyopathy and increased atrial fibrillation vulnerability.
CONCLUSIONS: These findings provide novel insights into underpinning mechanisms for atrial arrhythmia and a rationale for early P-wave changes in FD. These may be targeted to develop therapeutic strategies to reduce the arrhythmic burden in FD.

=== Abstract Y ===
BACKGROUND: Fabry disease (FD) is an X-linked lysosomal storage disorder caused by alpha-galactosidase A (alpha-Gal A) deficiency, leading to multiorgan accumulation of globotriaosylceramide that drives ventricular hypertrophy, fibrosis, inflammation, and arrhythmia. Atrial fibrillation (AF) is common in FD, yet the atrial cellular mechanisms that initiate this arrhythmic susceptibility, and whether they precede overt cardiomyopathy, remain undefined.

METHODS: We performed a translational study integrating three approaches. First, we analyzed ECG P-wave characteristics in a clinical cohort of 115 adults with FD spanning the spectrum of cardiomyopathy stages, compared with non-FD healthy controls. Second, we generated a human atrial cellular model by introducing the GLA p.N215S variant into induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs) using CRISPR-Cas9 genome editing and differentiating them into atrial cardiomyocytes (iPSC-CMs), benchmarked against isogenic wild-type (WT) iPSC-CMs for alpha-Gal A activity, globotriaosylceramide content, contraction, calcium handling, and electrophysiology. Third, we built bi-atrial in silico computational models incorporating the cellular electrophysiological and contractile changes measured in GLA p.N215S iPSC-CMs to test their impact on atrial conduction and AF vulnerability.

RESULTS: In the clinical cohort, FD adults showed shortening of P-wave duration and of the PQ interval that emerged before the onset of cardiomyopathy, together with a higher incidence of premature atrial contractions and an increased risk of AF compared with healthy controls. GLA p.N215S iPSC-CMs recapitulated the FD phenotype, being deficient in alpha-Gal A and accumulating globotriaosylceramide. Compared with WT, atrial GLA p.N215S iPSC-CMs exhibited a more positive diastolic membrane potential, a faster action potential upstroke velocity, a greater incidence of delayed afterdepolarizations, increased contraction force, and altered calcium handling. Bi-atrial in silico models embedding these cellular changes reproduced the P-wave morphology changes observed in early FD cardiomyopathy and predicted increased vulnerability to AF.

CONCLUSIONS: Atrial remodeling at the cellular level develops early in Fabry disease and provides a mechanistic basis for the P-wave changes and atrial arrhythmia that precede overt cardiomyopathy. By linking the GLA p.N215S variant to specific cellular electrophysiological and calcium-handling abnormalities, this integrated model identifies candidate therapeutic targets to reduce arrhythmic burden in FD.
