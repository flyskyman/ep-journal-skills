BACKGROUND: The radiofrequency balloon (RFB; HELIOSTAR, Biosense Webster) delivers energy to multiple electrodes simultaneously to improve the efficiency of pulmonary vein isolation relative to point-by-point radiofrequency ablation. However, which RFB ablation parameters predict durable, chronic scar remains undefined, leaving operators without validated targets for titrating energy delivery. We sought to identify the procedural parameters that determine chronic atrial scar formation with RFB ablation, using late gadolinium enhancement cardiac MRI (LGE-CMR) as the reference standard.

METHODS: In a chronic canine model (8 dogs), we performed circumferential RFB ablation with RF energy delivered to all electrodes simultaneously at four sites—superior vena cava, right superior pulmonary vein, left inferior pulmonary vein, and left atrial appendage—using ablation durations of 20 s or 60 s. Electroanatomic map ablation tags were projected onto LGE-CMR obtained 3 months after ablation and classified as ScarTags (correlated with CMR scar) or Non-ScarTags. Parameters associated with chronic scar were assessed by multivariate logistic regression.

RESULTS: Among 210 tags (80 superior vena cava, 80 right superior pulmonary vein, 20 left inferior pulmonary vein, 30 left atrial appendage), 168 were ScarTags and 42 Non-ScarTags. Lower electrode temperature before energy application was independently associated with chronic scar (OR 0.805, p=0.0075), as was longer RF duration (OR 2.360, p=0.0218). Impedance drop was not predictive (OR 0.986, p=0.373).

CONCLUSION: Lower pre-ablation electrode temperature and longer RF duration are the critical determinants of durable atrial scar with RFB ablation, whereas impedance drop is not. These exploratory findings suggest titrating these parameters may improve lesion durability and warrant clinical validation.
