Background: Approximately 30% of heart failure (HF) patients with left bundle branch block (LBBB) fail to respond to cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT), underscoring the need for better baseline predictors of non-response. We assessed whether baseline q waves in leads I, V5, or V6 predict CRT response in HF patients with LBBB.

Methods: We retrospectively enrolled a single cohort of 132 HF patients with left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) ≤35% and LBBB who underwent CRT implantation (mean age 63.0 ± 10.4 years; 94 [71.2%] male). Patients were stratified by the presence of any baseline q wave in leads I, V5, or V6: the qLBBB(+) group comprised 32 patients with such q waves, and the qLBBB(−) group comprised the remaining 100 patients without. Non-response to CRT was defined as LVEF improvement <5% at 6-month follow-up. Multivariable logistic regression was used to assess the independent association between baseline q waves and non-response.

Results: The CRT non-response rate was significantly higher in the qLBBB(+) group than in the qLBBB(−) group (68.8% vs 33.3%, p<.001). On multivariable logistic regression, the presence of baseline q waves in leads I, V5, or V6 was independently associated with higher CRT non-response (odds ratio 4.8, 95% confidence interval 1.5–15.0, p=.007).

Conclusions: The presence of any q wave in leads I, V5, or V6 is an independent predictor of CRT non-response in HF patients with LBBB. This simple baseline ECG marker may help identify candidates less likely to benefit from CRT and warrants prospective validation.
